WE MADE ELIUD KIPCHOGE RUN IN 0:56

Imagine yourself having to travel abroad, and try identify as a Kenyan to the world? What tricks have you used? Let us know when you remember. For those who are in the hustler nation of Kenya, what ways do you say you are Kenyan to those who call from beyond borders on your Zoom calls? Let us know when you remember. A lot of things have been able to define Kenyan’s, from tourism to agriculture, but if it’s one person who says Kenyan’s can keep and has a good sense of time, in our hustling and bustling of the city, it’s none other than our multiple record holder and famously 1:59 record holder, who says “No human is limited”, Eliud Kipchoge. A national banner to the world and an inspiration for many, from here in Kenya to vast reaches of the world, Kipchoge is the epitome of discipline and keeping his stature in midst of the world stage. A career that keeps long distance marathon runners on their toes and training programs to fill shoes, should he leave the stage. Coming to a time in his career, where, the questions of when he will leave the stage, starts to fly in the air, Void Atelier Productions and Waxxes animation studio wished to take a stab at being able to honour the national sporting hero. Through our own ways, we were able to think about how to craft a small trailer video that would serve as a stepping stone on how to capture Kipchoge and his strong ideals for discipline and self-belief.



Some pictures of Eliud Kipchoge

Inspired by the greenery of the Rift Valley region, we attempted to create an environment to capture the rural areas of their training and bring out the vast terrains in which the long distance runners use to sculpt their bodies for international competitions. As seen in the picture below. In addition to the environment, we wanted to keep his mannerism when running intact and show the hero in his best state.
